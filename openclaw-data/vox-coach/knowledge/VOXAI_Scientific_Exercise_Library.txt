## CORE USE RULES FOR VOXAI
1. Prescribe the smallest useful intervention.
2. In LISTENER MODE, choose 1 primary exercise for the biggest lever, then use the library to build the 7-day drill pack.
3. In STRATEGIST MODE, map top_issues from the JSON to the most relevant exercise category.
4. Use body-feel language: ribs, sternum, jaw, tongue, cheek buzz, back pressure, easy release, soft throat.
5. Never claim that a physiological mechanism is measured from audio unless it is directly audible. Use “likely”, “suggests”, or “inferred” when connecting a symptom to a cause.
6. No medical diagnosis. If pain, persistent hoarseness, voice loss, coughing blood, or severe fatigue is reported, recommend rest and qualified voice/medical support.
7. Exercises are not rewards. They are prescriptions. Give them only when connected to a symptom and a next-take goal.

## SAFETY GATES
- SOVT and gentle humming are first-line choices when strain, fatigue, or pressure is suspected.
- Avoid aggressive belting/high-intensity drills when audio shows strain, clipping, shouted tone, or fatigue.
- Do not use balloon/resistance drills if dizziness, breath-holding, or panic response appears.
- Do not prescribe extended high-range work for tired voices.
- Do not ask for volume as the fix; ask for coordination, space, support, or release.

## BREATH / ALIGNMENT / SUPPORT

### 1. The Farinelli Maneuver
- Use when: suspended breath control and phrase stamina.
- Pedagogical target: Suspended Breath Control.
- How to do it: Inhale for 4 counts, suspend (hold with open glottis) for 4, exhale for 4; increase to 10+ counts.
- How it should feel: Inhale low and wide, suspend without closing the throat, then release like a slow leak.
- Common failure / safety note: Stop or reduce intensity if dizziness, shoulder lifting, throat gripping, or breath-holding appears.
- Pass/Fail metric: Counts are even and the throat stays open during the suspension.
- Song transfer: Apply the same sensation to one short phrase only; do not run the whole song until the cue holds.

### 2. Rib Cage Stationary Drill
- Use when: rib stability and appoggio awareness.
- Pedagogical target: Thoracic Stability.
- How to do it: Place hands on lower ribs; inhale/exhale while keeping the ribs expanded and the sternum high.
- How it should feel: Keep the ribs gently open as if the body is staying wide around the sound.
- Common failure / safety note: Stop or reduce intensity if dizziness, shoulder lifting, throat gripping, or breath-holding appears.
- Pass/Fail metric: Ribs do not collapse at the start of the exhale.
- Song transfer: Apply the same sensation to one short phrase only; do not run the whole song until the cue holds.

### 3. The Sibilant Hiss
